Definition

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. While climate variations are natural, human activities since the 1800s have been the main driver of climate change, primarily due to burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas.

Why Climate Change Matters

üåæ

Food Security

Climate change affects crop yields, threatening food security in Ghana. Changes in rainfall patterns and rising temperatures impact agricultural productivity, especially for crops like cocoa, maize, and cassava.

Download PDF Guide
üíß

Water Resources

Changing precipitation patterns affect water availability. Ghana faces challenges with both droughts and flooding, impacting communities' access to clean water and affecting hydroelectric power generation.

Public Health

Climate change increases health risks through heat stress, vector-borne diseases like malaria, and air quality issues. Vulnerable populations, including children and elderly, are most at risk.

Causes of Climate Change

Fossil Fuel Burning

Coal, oil, and gas burning for electricity, heat, and transportation is the largest source of greenhouse gas emissions. In Ghana, this includes thermal power plants and increasing vehicle emissions.

Deforestation

How You Can Help

üö≤

Transportation

Walk, cycle, or use public transport. Consider carpooling and choose fuel-efficient vehicles. In Ghana, using tro-tros and shared taxis reduces individual carbon footprints.

üí°

Energy Conservation

Use LED bulbs, unplug devices when not in use, and choose energy-efficient appliances. Support Ghana's solar energy initiatives and consider solar panels for homes and schools.
